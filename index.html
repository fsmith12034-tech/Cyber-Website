My Personal Values

Ford Smith

Personal Values | Industry Page

1. Integrity

  1. Integrity is being honest and having strong moral principles, even when no one is watching.
  2. I learned the value of integrity from my father, who always emphasized accountability, especially when mistakes are made.
  3. It's important to me because it builds trust and respect in both personal and professional relationships.
  4. Without integrity, relationships become shallow, and it's hard to be honest with others or yourself.

2. Empathy

  1. Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of others.
  2. My mother valued empathy deeply and showed me how it helps connect with people of all backgrounds.
  3. It's important because it allows me to communicate with kindness and connect with others regardless of differences.
  4. Without empathy, relationships break down, and it's difficult to work with or relate to others. People feel isolated and unsupported.

3. Responsibility

  1. Responsibility means being reliable and owning your duties and actions.
  2. I learned responsibility early on by mowing lawns regularly and watching both of my parents prioritize their obligations above all else.
  3. It's important because it keeps life structured, helps meet goals, and earns the trust of others.
  4. Without responsibility, failure is almost certain — tasks get missed, and others can’t rely on you.

4. Growth

  1. Growth is the ongoing process of learning, improving, and becoming better through experience.
  2. My value of growth comes from my belief in self-respect — I feel validated only when I know I'm making progress.
  3. Growth matters because it keeps life fulfilling, helps me adapt, and gives me purpose.
  4. Without growth, life becomes boring and unfulfilling. It feels like you're stuck in place without reaching your potential.
